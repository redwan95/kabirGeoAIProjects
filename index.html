Project 01

Dual-Perspective Transit KPI Framework: Integrating GeoAI and Data-Driven Analysis for Urban Mobility in Greenville, SC

GeoAI Urban Mobility Travel Behavior Modeling GTFS Analysis NTD Metrics Transit Optimization Spatial Accessibility Network Efficiency GHG Emission Modeling Socio-Economic Disadvantage (SED) Mapping

Project Overview

This project, titled the Dual-Perspective Transit KPI Framework, integrates GeoAI and data-driven analysis to evaluate the efficiency and sustainability of urban mobility in Greenville, SC. By synthesizing GTFS spatial data with NTD operational metrics and Census socio-economic indicators, the framework implements complex travel behavior modeling and network performance evaluations. The research focuses on simulation and optimization of service delivery across Fixed-Route and Demand-Response Transit, utilizing geospatial joins and statistical regressions to quantify accessibility, environmental impact (GHG emissions), and cost-effectiveness. Key research themes include urban mobility optimization, accessibility modeling, spatial demographics, and transit equity analysis, providing a scalable toolkit for evidence-based transit planning and policy design.

Project 02

Northeast US Hurricane Vulnerability & Resilience Analysis: Building & Census Tract Level Analysis with Hurricane Intensity Variables

Big Data GeoAI Open Source Data Hurricane Resilience Spatial Analysis Machine Learning Vulnerability

Project Overview

This project integrates real-world big data from US Census ACS and TIGER/Line geospatial datasets with advanced GeoAI techniques, including Random Forest and SHAP for predictive modeling and spatial hotspot analysis, all within an open-source data framework, to provide a comprehensive hurricane vulnerability and resilience assessment.

Summary Output

The analysis revealed a compound vulnerability model, demonstrating that hurricane damage is driven by a complex interplay of factors. Key findings indicate that infrastructure age is a primary driver of vulnerability, with older buildings and pre-1960 housing significantly increasing damage risk. Mobility constraints, such as zero-vehicle households and public transit dependence, emerged as a critical new factor amplifying vulnerability, particularly in dense urban areas. The study also confirmed significant socio-economic disparities, showing that lower-income, higher-poverty, and older populations are disproportionately affected. A hierarchical regression model effectively quantified the cumulative impact of these factors, and advanced machine learning techniques, particularly Random Forest, achieved excellent predictive performance, identifying infrastructure age, sustained wind, and coastal proximity as top predictors. Finally, spatial hotspot analysis pinpointed specific geographic areas with concentrated damage clustering, highlighting regions in urgent need of resilience interventions.

Project 03

Urban Sentiment × Streetscape Quality: A GeoAI Analysis of Transit Accessibility and Emotional Experience in Fayetteville, AR

GeoAI Urban Mobility Sentiment Analysis Transit-Oriented Development (TOD) Streetscape Quality Spatial Statistics NLP in Planning

Project Overview

This research project employs GeoAI to map the intersection of urban sentiment and streetscape quality in Fayetteville, Arkansas, integrating BERT-based NLP on local texts with OSM infrastructure data. The findings demonstrate that green space and pedestrian path density are significantly stronger predictors of positive public sentiment (Adjusted R² = 0.407) than transit access alone, revealing that the emotional quality of urban mobility is heavily mediated by the physical streetscape. Through spatial clustering and K-Means typology, the analysis identifies critical inequality gaps in the transit network, suggesting that planning interventions must prioritize shaded, walkable environments to effectively improve the subjective experience of transit accessibility.

Project 04

Reconstructing Urban Mobility Resilience via GeoAI: The SF Dec 2025 Blackout

GeoAI Urban Mobility Crisis Informatics Spatial Analysis Autonomous Vehicles Infrastructure Resilience

Project Overview

This project utilizes GeoAI and Natural Language Processing (NLP) to reconstruct mobility disruptions during the December 2025 San Francisco power blackout. By synthesizing unstructured archival web data and verified news reports, the analysis maps the spatial and temporal impact of the outage on autonomous vehicle (AV) networks and public transit infrastructure. The study demonstrates how AI-driven spatial reconstruction can identify vulnerabilities in smart-city systems and inform urban resilience planning by bridging the data gap during critical infrastructure failures.

Summary Output

The analysis reveals that mobility disruptions significantly outlasted the initial power failure, with robotaxi service pauses occurring six hours post-incident and spatial clusters of stalled vehicles predominantly concentrated within 100 meters of non-functional traffic signals in the SoMa and Civic Center districts.

Project 05

AERO: Spatial Optimization and Climate Resilience of Renewable EV Infrastructure

GeoAI Urban Mobility EV Infrastructure GIS-MCDA Climate Resilience Renewable Energy Spatial Analytics Arkansas

Project Overview

This research develops the Arkansas EV Resilience Optimizer (AERO), a GeoAI-driven framework designed to strategically deploy renewable-powered charging stations. By utilizing GIS-MCDA (Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis), the project synthesizes high-resolution solar potential from NREL, climate hazard data from FEMA, and existing infrastructure density to solve complex urban mobility challenges. The methodology prioritizes expanding coverage to underserved rural regions while ensuring long-term operational cost-efficiency and infrastructure durability against extreme weather events.

Summary Output / Key Findings

  • Identified Lincoln, Phillips, and Cleveland as top-tier counties for infrastructure investment using a custom Deployment Priority Index (DPI).
  • Uncovered significant disparities in current access, with 57 counties (76%) currently lacking public charging stations.
  • Determined that 37 counties exceed the state's median climate risk, necessitating specialized grid-hardening for future mobility hubs.
  • Established a strong correlation (R² = 0.62) between solar potential and suitability, validating renewable energy as a key driver for sustainable mobility.
